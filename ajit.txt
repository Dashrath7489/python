Ajit Pawars grandparents were Govindrao and Sharda Pawar.[6][7] Govindrao was associated with worker cooperatives in Baramati, while Sharda managed the family farm.[6] In 1938, she was elected to the Pune local board.[7] The couple had eleven children, including seven sons and four daughters;[7] one of their sons, named Sharad Pawar, later became the president of the Nationalist Congress Party (NCP) and a four-time chief minister of Maharashtra.[6]Later, he was elected to the Maharashtra Legislative Assembly seven times from the Baramati Assembly constituency. He first won in a 1991 by-election and subsequently retained it for five consecutive terms in 1995, 1999, 2004, 2009, and 2014.
